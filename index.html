About Me

I am a PhD student studying mathematics at the University of British Columbia under the supervision of Sébastien Picard. I obtained my Bachelor of Mathematics from the University of Waterloo, where I held USRAs under Benoit Charbonneau and Laurent Marcoux. I also got my Master of Mathematics at the University of Waterloo, with Spiro Karigiannis as my supervisor.

My main research interests are in differential geometry and geometric analysis, especially with regard to manifolds with special holonomy.

A copy of my CV can be found here.

Research

My research interests mostly lie in differential geometry and geometric analysis. More recently I have been working with geometric flows on manifolds with special holonomy, such as the Laplacian flow and coflow on G2 manifolds and the anomaly flow on complex 3-folds.
